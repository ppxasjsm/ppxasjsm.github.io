Research

My main research interest lies in the development and application of numerical simulation methods to biomolecular problems. In particular, I work on problems related to protein-ligand binding, molecular recognition, and being able to reliably predict free energies of binding and interaction residence times. I draw from a rich background in statistical physics, mathematical modeling, Bayesian inference, and Machine Learning.

Software

As part of the ongoing research work I have conributed to a varied of software. Below is a selection of most recent and notable contributions.

BioSimSpace

BioSimSpace is the Flagship Software Development Project for CCP-BioSim, and involves international and industrial partners. It provides an interoperability layer to allow different software packages used for biomolecular simulations to be combined into workflows for applications such as modelling drug binding. See the documentation for jupyter notebooks for some examples.

pyFEAnalysis (freenrgworkflows)

A tool for the analysis of alchemical free energy simulations generated with Sire or BioSimSpace

Science Communication

IMAGINARY is an open source platform for mathematics communication resources. It is a non-profit organisation that started as the mathematics outreach project of the Mathematische Forschungsinstitut Oberwolfach (MFO). Currently, it hosts two mathematics related exhibitions (“IMAGINARY - through the eyes of mathematics” and “Math of Planet Earth”), but also interactive programs, films etc. that can be used in maths classes at school or at home. My involvement with IMAGINARY started, when a film in collaboration with Guillaume Jouvet and Chantalle Landry was awarded the third prize in the "mathematics for planet earth competition" in 2013. Since then the winning film has been shown internationally at various different museums and events, for example, the MiMa museum Oberwolfach, the Technische Museum in Berlin and the ICM in Seoul. The film addresses the mathematics needed to model the flow of ice in a glacier.

Since then, a second film based on Guillaume's research has won the first prize of the fast forward science initiative hosted on youtube.

Since 2014, I have been worked as a freelancer for IMAGINARY. As part of the work, I have lead exhibition setups and workshops in many places around the world, for example at the Weizmann Institute in Israel, Dar Es Salam in Tanzania and at the AIMS centre in Cape Town South Africa. I also lead workshops, for example, a UNESCO funded teacher training event in rural Liberia and most recently I was involved with an exhibition in Bucharest and the second MPE exhibition at Imperial College London, where the second round of MPE competition winners were announced.
